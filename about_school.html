Human Computer Interaction

HCI was all about determining a need, creating a low-fidelity product, getting user feedback and then using that feedback to create a high-fidelity product. I really enjoyed this class and felt like the class offered the most growth. I think this is primarily because user testing and feedback is not something we do a lot in my role as a designer at work. Much of the feedback we receive is from our learners by way of an L1 survey and we rely on the learner's performance metrics to tell us how effective the course was at teaching the learner. After this class, I brought this up to my team and suggested that we implement a process that allows for more testing and feedback because it is something I'm excited about.

Integration of Educational Technology

This class has been helpful for me as I've been working the CEVO process at work. One of the biggest things this class helped me with was developing new ideas for activities that employ different types of technology. Additionally, in this class we were given the opportunity to reflect on existing tools, such as websites, applications, and other technological devices to determine their effectiveness and safety for a learner. I really appreciated these assignments because it introduced me to a decision making matrix that I think I may be able to use on a local committee that I serve on. Finally, I was given the opportunity to identify IEST standards that I'm already practicing and some that I should develop. Over the long run, having an awareness of and developing skills to operate on these standards will help me be a better educator, leader and designer.
